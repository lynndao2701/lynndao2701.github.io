## Overview

This project analyses why Starbucks' CRM and data systems failed during its 2008 downturn and proposes a modern Business Intelligence–driven solution to improve personalisation, customer retention, and operational efficiency. Using BI concepts, CRM analysis, and Power BI dashboards, it demonstrates how data-driven insights can transform customer engagement and support better decision-making in a global retail environment.

Completed for **BUSA3002 – Business Intelligence & Data Management** at the University of Newcastle.

## What I Did

- Analysed CRM capability gaps across personalisation, retention, forecasting, and real-time reporting using requirements analysis and gap analysis
- Designed a BI architecture integrating CRM, POS, ERP, ETL, and data warehouse components to support a scalable, future-state reporting solution
- Built Power BI dashboards covering customer segmentation (RFM and clustering), AI-driven product recommendations (collaborative filtering and contextual rules), and customer retention/churn (logistic regression concepts and sentiment analysis)
- Proposed a real-time CRM and ETL/streaming pipeline design, and a time-series approach to demand forecasting for inventory optimisation

## Tools & Techniques

Power BI, Python (ML concepts), ETL pipeline design, data warehouse and OLAP, RFM analysis, CRM analysis, conceptual NLP

## Outcome

The proposal demonstrated how a BI-enhanced CRM could address the data and personalisation gaps that contributed to Starbucks' 2008 decline, providing a practical framework for real-time insights and stronger customer retention.
